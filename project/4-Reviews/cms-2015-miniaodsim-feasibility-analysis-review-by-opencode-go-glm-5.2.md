## Executive Summary

The document is a careful, well-structured feasibility analysis that correctly concludes **CONDITIONAL GO**. Its strongest points are the direct remote ROOT inspection (not reliance on documentation), the explicit pretraining-overlap framing, the honest `ANALYZED`/`VERIFICATION LIMITATION` admission that no CMSSW event loop was run, and the frozen corpus integrity snapshot (event/file/byte totals were re-checked and are internally consistent: 113,275,027 events, 2,789 files, 3.542 TiB).

The blocking issues are concentrated in four areas that must be resolved before the research plan is revised and E0 begins: (1) **no cross-section/sample weighting rule for combining multiple QCD generator-level bins**; (2) **the 10⁵ signal-jet target is supported only by a pre-truth-matching candidate rate, threatening the RQ2 `n_max` denominator**; (3) **jet-energy-correction level, impact-parameter reference points, units, and packing scale are unspecified**; and (4) **the deterministic split rule hashes an undefined "canonical PFN" with weak 32-bit bucketing**. Several Medium/Low findings address PID vocabulary, pileup, bin type (HT vs pThat), reproducibility artifacts, and clarity/consistency with the parent research plan.

## Overall Verdict

**Conditional approval, pending revision.** The document is methodologically sound, internally consistent in its arithmetic (event/file/byte totals verified), and commendably honest about the absence of a CMSSW event loop. However, before it can drive a revision of `research-plan-v0.3.md` or unblock E0, the Critical and High findings must be addressed in the document itself and carried into the revised plan:

1. Define QCD cross-section/sample weighting across HT bins (Critical).
2. Produce a matched-*signal* yield estimate and reconcile it with the RQ2 `n_max = 10⁵` denominator (High).
3. Specify the jet-energy-correction level and re-baseline the §8.1 rate against corrected pT (High).
4. Pin impact-parameter reference points, units (cm), packing scale, and the transform convention (High).
5. Define signal-match ambiguity and the generator-status traversal convention (High).
6. Define "canonical PFN" and strengthen the split rule's bucketing and balance estimate (Medium).

The Medium/Low/Info findings (PID vocabulary, pileup, bin type, reproducibility artifacts, leakage-criterion alignment, two-environment pinning) should be resolved in the same revision but are not individually blocking.
