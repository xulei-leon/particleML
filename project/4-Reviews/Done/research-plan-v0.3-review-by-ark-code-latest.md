## 1. Executive Assessment

The v0.3 plan is a substantial and mostly successful refinement of v0.2. The core scientific
move — reframing the study from a "test-time feature masking" diagnostic to a "fine-tune
without X" feature-availability study with nested feature configurations A–D — is correct,
well argued, and gives the project a defensible causal boundary. The claim hierarchy in
§3 is unusually disciplined for an undergraduate plan and directly supports the applicant
narrative (a first-author-style paper with careful uncertainty reporting is a much stronger
PhD application artifact than a broad, under-controlled benchmark).

Feasibility is broadly plausible under the stated constraints (personal cloud GPU, no
institutional cluster, JetClass storage), but several concrete numbers are missing or
under-specified: no GB or GPU-hour estimates, no explicit choice of pretrained checkpoint,
no data-download / storage plan, and no fallback if the pretrained backbone is unavailable
or non-releasable. These are the highest-leverage gaps to close before E0.

The plan is well aligned with the applicant narrative and the researcher's current skill
mix (Python, scientific computing, Bayesian MCMC → paired bootstrap, DeLong, run-record
discipline are natural extensions). The biggest risks are: (a) the pretrained checkpoint
identity is unspecified and load-compatibility is treated as a solved problem when it is
usually the hardest engineering step; (b) "Config D" is intentionally left partly open
until E0, which is defensible but currently under-guarded against label leakage; and (c)
the timeline is optimistic given a full-time undergraduate courseload.

**Bottom line:** approve to proceed with E0, subject to resolving the Critical and High
findings below (checkpoint identity, storage/compute numbers, Config-D freeze procedure,
and a written adapter-loading spike).

## 2. Strengths (for context only, not action items)

- Correct and precise framing shift from test-time masking to fine-tune-without-X (§0, §6).
- Nested feature ladder A ⊂ B ⊂ C ⊂ D with explicit exclusion of truth-level variables (§5).
- Explicit claim hierarchy with "avoid these claims" list (§3).
- Data-efficiency statistic defined as a formula, not a vibe (§2 RQ2).
- Uncertainty plan names three complementary layers (seed, bootstrap, paired) and requires
  saved per-event predictions (§9).
- Run-record schema in §10 is concrete enough to implement directly.
- Scope ladder and decision gates (§11, §12) are the right shape for budget discipline.
- Minimum-viable-paper checklist in §14 maps 1:1 to the RQs.

## 4. Summary by Category

- **Critical (2):** pretrained checkpoint identity is unspecified; adapter-loading feasibility is
  assumed rather than verified. Both should be resolved before E1.
- **High (7):** compute/storage numbers absent; Config D leakage procedure absent; input-adapter
  parametric form unspecified; `normalized_gap_closed` under-defined; PET-vs-OmniLearn
  inconsistency; JetClass storage/download plan missing; timeline lacks calendar anchoring.
- **Medium (9):** analysis endpoints, uncertainty specifics, run-record schema gaps, baseline
  choice concreteness, and sensitivity-check definitions.
- **Low / Info (7):** naming, forward references, venue selection, and low-cost applicant-fit
  additions (Bayesian appendix, Zenodo deposit).

## 5. Recommendations Before Approving E0

1. Freeze the pretrained checkpoint (name, version, hash, license, backup).
2. Insert an E0.5 adapter-loading spike gate between E0 and E1.
3. Write §11.1 with concrete GPU-hour, GB, and dollar estimates; add a hard dollar cap.
4. Define the Config-D leakage acceptance test.
5. Pick one architecture name (PET-style *or* OmniLearn-style) and apply it consistently.
6. Add data acquisition + storage subsection to §4.
7. Anchor the timeline to a specific calendar window and hours/week assumption.

None of the findings undermine the scientific direction. The plan is sound; the remaining
work is engineering realism (checkpoint, compute, storage) and tightening two under-specified
methods details (Config D freeze procedure, input-adapter form).
