**Overall assessment:** The sprint correctly identifies the no-host/no-push constraint and the need to defer qualified-host execution. However, the scope definition is internally contradictory, the file-structure table lists deliverables that do not exist in the codebase and are not acknowledged as entirely absent, and work-package items that require the qualified host are interleaved with local-only items without clear demarcation. The introduction of a new `e0.py` module and `e0-audit.schema.json` lacks an articulated boundary with the existing `audit.py` module and is not present in the specification or development plan.

## Summary

| Severity | Count |
|---|---|
| Critical | 4 |
| High | 4 |
| Medium | 6 |
| Low | 4 |
| Info | 2 |

The sprint document correctly anchors its scope to the approved requirements, specification, and research plan. It accurately records the no-host/no-push constraint and identifies the deferred qualified-host gate. The primary deficiencies are:

1. **Scope ambiguity** — the "Included"/"Not included" sections mix full-sprint-intent items with this-iteration-deliverable items, and several checklist items implicitly assume host access that does not exist.

2. **Missing-codebase acknowledgment** — the file-structure table in §4 lists deliverables that are entirely absent from the repository, without stating that status.

3. **Undefined module boundary** — `e0.py` vs `audit.py` and `e0-audit.schema.json` vs procedural audit introduce new artifacts without justifying the split against the existing codebase or specification.

4. **Work-package ambiguity** — §5.2 and §5.3 interleave local-schema tasks with host-only tasks under the same work-package heading, creating unclear actionability.

These issues are addressable through document revision without requiring qualified-host access. The sprint should be revised to clearly delimit local vs deferred scope before implementation begins.
